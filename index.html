Balls to the Wall

Balls to the Wall was an 4-player local arcade sports game, I helped develop during my final year of my Game Development program, as part of the capstone class.

For this project, I was put into a team of seven, and had to work with two other programmers to help create the game. For my contribution to the games, I programed the obstacles, power-ups, audio system and in-game debug system. However, due to testing of the game play, we decided to not have obstacles and power-ups in the game as it affected the gameplay flow. On top of those contributions, I also helped test and fix bugs within the game.

In the end, we had 3 months to design the game, and 3 months to develop the game. And at the end, we showcased it at the Level Up Showcase, a Ontario college and university game development competition, where the game was a big hit, finishing in the top 5 out of over 80 games.

Roadblock

Roadblock is a mobile single player puzzle game, where the player has to move the key piece out of the playing area, by moving other pieces.

Every asset created for this game was created by myself, from the pieces to the scripts.

The game only has 5 scenes, the main menu, the settings, the credits, the level selection and finally the main game. Within the main game a script gets what level number the user selects, and loads the level using a prefab, meaning every level runs in the same screen!
